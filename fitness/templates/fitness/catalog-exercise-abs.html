Catalog of Exercises: Abs

Equipment Required

Cable Machine Crunches
1. Grab a rope attachment with both hands and have your thumbs facing up.

 2. Kneel and position the rope behind your head. Both hands should be close to your neck throughout the exercise.

3. Once in this position, engage your glutes to produce some degree of posterior pelvic tilt and to engage your abs better.

4. Initiate the crunch by inhaling and bringing the rope down and in. Think of the movement as one where you actively crunch and extend your abs instead of having the movement go through your hips.

5. Hold the contraction for a moment, exhale, and extend your abs until your back is in a neutral position.
Glute Hamstring Situp
1. Set up the apparatus so that when you're sitting in the machine, heels against foot pads (toes pointing up), your glutes are off the pad. With feet in the pads, sit up tall, and bend your knees.

2. Keep a tight core and extend backward until your back is parallel with the floor.

3. Return to a seated position by aggressively flexing your quads (aka straightening your legs) and reach your hands forward.
Incline Bench Situp
1. Hook feet under support and lie supine on an incline bench with hips bent.

2. Place hands behind the neck or on the side of the neck.

3. Raise the torso from the bench by bending your waist and hips.

4. Return until the back of shoulders contact the incline board.
Rowing Machine Knee Tuck
1. Get into a push-up position with your feet on the seat of the rower.

2. Slowly raise your hips up in the air until your hips and legs are in the pike position.

3. Slowly lower back to the starting position.
Pullup Bar Knee Raise
1. Dead Hang from a pull-up bar or gymnastics rings with a grip that's comfortable, with your arms and legs fully extended, and feet off the ground.

2. Raise your knees up towards your chest as high as possible.

3. Hold for a brief moment and slowly return to the starting position.
No Equipment Required

Crunch
1. Lie down on your back. Plant your feet on the floor, hip-width apart. Bend your knees and place your arms across your chest. Contract your abs and inhale.

2. Exhale and lift your upper body, keeping your head and neck relaxed.

3. Inhale and return to the starting position.
Crunch
1. Lie down on your back. Bend your knees and plant your feet on the floor, hip-width apart. Place your arms behind your head, pointing your elbows outward.

2. Brace your abs. Lift your knees to 90 degrees and raise your upper body. This is your starting position.

3. Exhale and rotate your trunk, moving your right elbow and left knee toward each other. Simultaneously straighten your right leg. Pause.

4. Inhale and return to the starting position.

5. Exhale. Move your left elbow to your right knee and extend your left leg. Pause. This completes 1 rep.
Reverse Crunch
1. Lie face-up on a mat or other soft surface with your knees bent at 90 degrees and your feet flat on the floor. Keep your arms near your sides with your palms down.

2. Exhale and brace your core. Lift your feet off the ground and raise your thighs until they’re vertical. Keep your knees bent at 90 degrees throughout the movement.

3. Tuck your knees toward your face as far as you can comfortably go without lifting your mid-back from the mat. Your hips and lower back should lift off the ground.

4. Hold for a moment and slowly lower your feet back toward the floor until they reach the ground.

Heel taps
1. Lay, back down, on the floor with your head and shoulders raised looking between your legs. Knees should be bent with the soles of the feet flat on the floor.

2. Alternate touching each ankle with your hands by moving from side to side keeping your head and shoulders raised off the floor.
Planks
1. Get into a push up position, with your elbows under your shoulders and your feet hip-width apart.

2. Bend your elbows and rest your weight on your forearms and on your toes, keeping your body in a straight line.

3. Hold for as long as possible.
Up-down planks
1. Begin in an elbow plank position, propped on your elbows and supporting your weight on your toes.

2. Shift your weight to your left and place your right hand on the floor.

3. Shift your weight to the right and place your left hand on the floor to enter a full plank.

4. Drop back down the way you came. Move your weight to the right to put your left elbow on the floor. Then shift to the left and place your right elbow back on the floor.

5. Finish the set then switch, this time placing your left hand on the floor first, then your right.
Side planks
1. Lie on your right side, legs extended and stacked from hip to feet. The elbow of your right arm is directly under your shoulder. Ensure your head is directly in line with your spine. Your left arm can be aligned along the left side of your body.

2. Engage your abdominal muscles, drawing your navel toward your spine.

3. Lift your hips and knees from the mat while exhaling. Your torso is straight in line with no sagging or bending. Hold the position.

4. After several breaths, inhale and return to the starting position.
Mountain climbers
1. Start on all fours, hands shoulder-width apart and knees hip-width apart. Brace your core.

2. Move your right thigh toward your chest and place your toes on the floor. Straighten your left leg behind you, flex your foot, and place it on the floor.

3. Swiftly switch legs without moving your arms.
Leg Raises
1. Lie on your back, legs straight and together.

2. Keep your legs straight and lift them all the way up to the ceiling until your butt comes off the floor.

3. Slowly lower your legs back down till they’re just above the floor. Hold for a moment.

4. Raise your legs back up.
